Research (* denotes equal contribution)
International Survey Preparation for Brain-Computer Interface Research
Zichen Zhang, Mariska Vansteensel, Betts Peters, Jane E. Huggins
University of Michigan UROP 2023 Spring Research Symposium,  2023 

Brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) is a technology that allows a human brain and an external device to exchange signals. As the BCI field matures, the implementation of BCIs for people with significant motor disability often reveals unexpected factors affecting system performance and user satisfaction. These factors can be intrinsic (related to some characteristic of the user, such as disease etiology) or extrinsic (environmental factors, such as time of day). This study is planned to find out BCI researchers‚Äô opinions on these factors based on the International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health (ICF) framework. Implemented using Qualtrics, this study plans to survey members BCI community on the reporting of 19 ICF categories of intrinsic and extrinsic factors in their use of BCIs by recording (1) the demographic information of BCI researchers, (2) the general information of their research participants, and (3) researchers‚Äô opinions on their top 5 most important ICF categories of extrinsic and intrinsic factors in terms of the effects they may have on BCI performance and user satisfaction. Our findings are still undetermined because we are still in the process of distributing the survey and collecting data. Our study aims to find out some of the most important factors BCI researchers take into account during their studies and the scientific reasons behind them. The results of the survey can help us determine the most relevant extrinsic and intrinsic factors impacting BCI research around the world. Our conclusion will help the BCI community reach consensus about which factors are the most important and assist the community in capturing and reporting on these factors in a consistent manner.
